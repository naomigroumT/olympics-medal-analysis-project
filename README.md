# Winter-Olympics-Medal-Data-Analysis
I analyzed Winter Olympics medal data from 1924 to 2014 to compare hosting effects, identify sport-specific dominance, and examine how the global distribution of medal-winning countries had changed over time.
## Research Questions: 
- Is there a correlation between being from the host country and whether or not an athlete wins a medal? Does home advantage actually show up in the data? 
- Are there any countries that are dominant in a specific sport? And are these the same countries that dominate overall, or do smaller nations punch above their weight in certain events?
- Has the global distribution of medals changed over time? Are traditional powerhouses still dominating, or are new countries emerging as competitors?
## Key Findings 
I found that countries tend to win more medals in the years they host the Winter Olympics, showing a clear hosting advantage. In addition, I discovered that speed skating has the largest medal pool, and the Netherlands, despite being a smaller overall medal nation, dominates this sport. Finally, I observed that the Winter Olympics have become more global over time, with more countries winning medals, even though traditional powerhouses still lead overall.
